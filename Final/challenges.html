Sleep for 30 days straight

For the next 30 days maintain a stable sleep schedchule. The specific amount of sleep does not matter as much as going to sleep and waking up at the same time, however aiming to sleep for at least 8 hours a night woukd be ideal.

The Impossible Pull Up

The rules for this one are pretty simple - you have to do one pull up. The trick is that the pull up has to last for at least 2 minutes (you can go for 4 minutes if you are a bit more advanced). This means that you have to go up for at least for a full minute and go down for the same amount. Good luck!

The Impossible Push Up

After completing the impossible pull up challenge you may feel like this one is going to be a walk in the park. This is exactly why the minimum time required for the push up to count is 4 minutes - 2 down and 2 going up. Good luck!

30 Days of Push Ups

Yet another challenge that includes push ups. This one, however, is a bit more long term. In order to complete this challenge you are required to do 100 push ups every single day. If that is too easy for you you can either increase the amount to 200 (going any more than that will not give you any benefits) or what I would go for try to do some harder push-up variations such as: explosive push ups, diamond push ups, pyke push ups, handstand pushups.

A Month of Cold Showers

The challenge is pretty simple: you just have to shower for thirty days straight. While using the coldest water you can handle, thought, anything less than the coldest it can go would feel like cheating, right? Besides many health benefits cold showers also help us prepare for the flinch phenomenon, which if you are interested in learning more about, you should read up on.

Counting Calories

While dieting or restricting yourself from certain foods such as sugar (which I also encourage you to try out for at least a month) could certainly make for a good challenge, for this one you will not be required to do any of that. All you have to do is count every single calorie that you put inside your body for the next 30 days. Sometimes learning just a little bit more about what we do daily on autopilot can heavily influence our actions and decisions.

Sticking to it

Probably the hardest part about anything in life is the beggining. Especcialy when you are trying to improve your life by starting a diet of trying to finally start going to the gym. That is what this challenge is all about. For the next 30 days you need to choose something that have been wanting to do for a while but kept putting off and apply a two day rule to it - you can take as many days off as you wish, but you cannot take any more than 1 day off in a row. This means if you felt too tired to do it today, then the following day, no matter how you feel you must go through with the activity that you have chosen.

Bring Sally Up

If you have not heard of this challenge before, just make a quick search on youtube and follow allong. When sally goes up you go up as well and push youself off then ground. When Sally goes down you dont just plop on the ground but lower yourself to a plank position and hold it until Sally goes up once again. No raising your butts! Also if you want to you can try swapping out push ups for squats, pull ups, dips etc.

The Magical Push Up Challenge

In order to do this challenge you have to do a single clean push up. However, the push up must be done while holding onto one side of the bar while the other side is resting on top of an elevated surface. In order to complete the challenge you must have clean form and try your best to have as much bar floating as possible, while also keeping both of your hands away from the supporting surface.
